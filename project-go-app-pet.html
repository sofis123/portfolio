Booking a Dog Walker

Client - Go App pet

Project Overview

When hiring a service for pets, customers want to know better who they are trusting their lovely animals. Working as a UX Designer at Go App pet, a startup based in Brazil, I could rethink the experience of booking a dog walk, creating a better connection between pet owners and dog walkers, and giving them more proximity and interaction. My challenge as UX Designer was to improve the interaction between pet owners and professionals, bringing trust to customers and increasing clients for dog walkers.

ROLE

UX Researcher, UX Designer & Interaction Designer

TOOLS

Invision, Sketch, Miro, Marvel, Google Forms & Sticky Notes

METHODS

Market research, Survey, Interviews, User flow, Wireframes, Moodboard, Competitive Analysis, Affinity Mapping, User Journey & Agile Development

DURATION

1 week UX and 1 week UI

Research

Following the design thinking approach, where research always comes first, I started applying a survey to gather quantitative and qualitative data about user behaviors regarding dog walker experience. I collected 34 responses and interviewed 7 people (dog owners and dog walkers) to find out more about their challenges.

Key Insights

I wrote the most significant findings from the research in sticky notes and grouped them accordingly to identify pain points and define a clear problem to solve, the results were:

  • Dog owners need to have trust in the dog walker.
  • Dog owners wants a dog walker with experience.
  • Dog owners like to receive updates about their dogs.

User Flow

The user flow shows the difference between the dog owner and dog walker flow to book and executes the dog walk service. Today the current app has no differentiation between them and identifying this helped me to improve it thinking about the different experiences for each persona.

Feedback & Next steps…

After all this process I could observe that conducting a usability test with the first version of a prototype, after testing with users, I was able to understand that the current flow was not efficient, and users were looking for more information before hiring a service. Based on data and tests I could come up with a solution to better connect users and professionals which was the main pain point we discovered during research. Now this project is presenting as a prototype for future investors to raise funds to build the 2.0 product. During the process, I had the developers on my side looking and validating the solution in order to build a viable product.
